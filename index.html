The grid never sleeps. Scroll to walk it.

01

FIRST STOP

Noodle Row

Steam curls off street carts under a ceiling of cable and signage. Broth prices flicker with the currency exchange, updated every few minutes by vendors who trust the market more than the weather. Nobody here asks where the meat came from.

By 2 a.m. the row is louder than daylight ever gets — bowls clacking, static radio, a dozen dialects arguing over the last table.

02

SECOND STOP

The Data Spire

Ninety floors of leased server racks cool themselves with river water pumped up through the building's spine. Couriers ride the service elevators with drives instead of packages. Nobody who works here has seen the penthouse — that floor answers to different owners entirely.

At night the spire's cooling vents fog the lower windows, so from the street it looks like the building is breathing.

03

THIRD STOP

The Underbelly

Below the transit deck, sunlight is a rumor. Shops here run on generators and reputation, trading parts, favors, and information in roughly equal measure. The rules are simpler underground: pay what you owe, and don't ask a vendor where they slept last.

Half the district's rumors start in this half-mile of tunnel — and most of them turn out true.

04

FOURTH STOP

Skyline Exchange

The exchange floor sits on a rooftop garden nobody waters — the plants are synthetic, kept for the view they give the traders below. Fortunes here move at the speed of light between towers, decided by people who never once look up from the glass.

From this height the whole district is just light: a circuit board with a few million tenants wired into it.

END OF LINE

Terminus reached. The district goes quiet — until the next cycle.
